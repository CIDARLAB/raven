Description of Assembly Method

In BioBricks, all parts are preceded by EcoRI and XbaI restriction sites, and followed by SpeI and PstI. Digesting these restriction sites produces common sticky ends, which allow 2 different BioBrick parts and their desitination vector to be assembled.

Shetty, R. P., Endy, D. & Knight, T. F. Engineering BioBrick vectors from BioBrick parts. Journal of Biological Engineering 2 (2008).

Description of Assembly Method

CPEC (Circular Polymerase Extension Cloning) is a one step multiway assembly method. Multiple double stranded DNA parts with overlapping sequences can be denatured and amplified using DNA polymermase. The final concatenated part must have homology on both ends with the assembly vector for a plasmid to form properly.

Quan, J. & Tian, J. Circular Polymerase Extension Cloning of Complex Gene Libraries and Pathways. PLoS ONE 4, e6441 (2009).

Description of Assembly Method

Gibson is a one-pot, isothermal, multi-way assembly method. Gibson assembly relies on the combined effects of a 5' exonuclease, DNA polymerase, and a DNA ligase to assemble overlapping DNA molecules. In the first part of the reaction, overhangs between two overlapping parts are created by the exonuclease, which allows the two parts to anneal. The gaps on each strand of the annealed parts is then repaired with DNA polymerase and DNA ligase.

Gibson, D. G. et al. Enzymatic assembly of DNA molecules up to several hundred kilobases. Nature Methods 6 (2009).

Description of Assembly Method

Golden Gate is a one step multi-way assembly method that relies on Type IIs restriction enzymes. The derivative method applied here uses a tiered destination vector system, using restriction enzymes BbsI and BsaI. Fusions sites are selected between two adjacent parts to produce a scarless assembly. Parts are digested with Type IIs restriction enzymes and ligated in a one-pot reaction. This method assumes the acquisition of the LacZ fragment necessary for blue-white screening and the vector pSK1A2.

Engler, C., Kandzia, R. & Marillonnet, S. A One Pot, One Step, Precision Cloning Method with High Throughput Capability. PLoS ONE 3 (2008).

Description of Assembly Method

MoClo is a one step multi-way assembly method that uses common overhang junctions generated by type IIs restriction enzymes. Parts are digested with Type IIs restriction enzymes and ligated in a one-pot reaction. Although the common overhang junctions are effectively assembly scars, the use of common junctions allows the reuse of parts, which reduces the cost. Parts are digested with Type IIs restriction enzymes and ligated in a one-pot reaction. This method assumes the acquisition of the LacZ fragment necessary for blue-white screening and the vector pSB1A2 and pSB1K3.

Weber, E., Engler, C., Gruetzner, R., Werner, S. & Marillonet, S. A Modular Cloning System for Standardized Assembly of Multigene Constructs. PloS ONE 6 (2010).

Description of Assembly Method

SLIC (Sequence and Ligation Independent Cloning) is a one-pot multi-way assembly methods taht uses in vitro homologous recombination and single-strand annealing. SLIC uses exonuclease to generate single stranded DNA overhangs in inserts as well as vectors. SLIC effectively mimics in vivo homologous recombination.
